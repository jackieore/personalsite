MENTORSHIP

I want to share my skills with those younger than me in hopes that I can inspire other girls of color to pursue technology. In 2009, I co-founded a STEM camp for young girls and throughout high school I helped facilitate and organize seven camps. I have been a technical mentor with Black Girls Code since March 2014. I believe that through early technology education we can change the face of through technology industry by showing underrepresented students that they have the power to be creators.

TECHNOLOGY

I'm passionate about education and technology. Knowledge is power and coding is an outlet to create technology that makes positive impacts on communities. I built, designed, and launched a book resource application called We Read Too. I was a mobile development intern at Time Inc during the summer of 2014 and for the summer of 2015 I was a Code2040 fellow working on Intuit's Mint iOS team. On Dartmouth's campus I have done programming with Tiltfactor, DALI Lab and worked for the Computer Science department. I started a YouTube channel Code With Kaya which features tech tutorials, vlogs, and tech resources tips.

WE READ TOO




WHAT IS WE READ TOO?

We Read Too is a cultural literacy application that showcases a directory of over 350 Children's and Young Adult fiction books written by authors of color for people of color. You can browse different genres, search by author or title, view the details of every book, suggest any books to be added, as well as view the book on the web through Safari. This app is very important to me because I've always loved reading from the moment I learned how to, but I constantly found that I couldn’t relate to the characters in popular books. I created this application so diverse books can be showcased in one central place where readers can find books with diverse characters they can relate to. The app currently has over 4,000 users and has received great ratings in the App Store. Available for free in the iTunes App Store.
